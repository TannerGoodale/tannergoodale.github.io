Kansas City Missouri Temple

The Kansas City Missouri Temple was the second temple built in Missouri. It stands in an area ripe with Church History and has unique architecture for the area. In 2012, it won the Precast/Prestressed Concrete Institute's Design Award for Best Religious Structure.

Laie Hawaii Temple

The Laie Hawaii Temple is the first temple to be built in the Pacific. It was initially dedicated in 1919 by Heber J. Grant and was rededicated most recently in 2010 by Thomas S. Monson. The exterior is made from native crushed lava rock and coral, reinforced with steel.
